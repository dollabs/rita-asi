<h1>What you see</h1>

Genesis reads a short, simple version of Macbeth&mdash;about 100 lines&mdash;and constructs an <i>elaboration graph</i>, 
which consists of explicit events (in white) and inferences (in other colors) inserted using common sense rules.
Once the elaboration graph is constructed, Genesis searches for instances of concept patterns such as <i>Revenge</i>.

<p>
Then, Genesis summarizes the story in various ways, with the most compact summary based on the story elements that
are involved in the discovered concept that involve the most story elements.
